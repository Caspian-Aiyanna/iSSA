Study Overview

BACI Design

Before-After-Control-Impact study examining elephant behavior across fence removal periods: Pre, Interim, and Post phases.

HMM Decoding

Hierarchical Hidden Markov Models (HMM) classify trajectories into binary states: Sleeping, Low-energy, Foraging, and Movement, with spatial interactions (Bounce).

RSF Analysis

Resource Selection Functions model habitat selection intensity, revealing behavioral-specific landscape preferences.

Research Framework

A multi-scale investigation into elephant behavioral shifts and spatial constraints following landscape-level management interventions.

RQ 1

Overall Activity Budgets

How do African elephants allocate time among movement, foraging, sleeping, and low-energy behavioral states as inferred from GPS collar data?

Predictions:
  • Primary allocation to low-energy and foraging states; movement and sleeping comprising smaller fractions.
  • Seasonal variance: Increased movement during resource-limited periods vs. low-energy behavior during resource-rich periods.
  • Sleeping states spatially and temporally clustered (predominantly night/low-disturbance zones).
RQ 2

Spatial Distribution (RSF)

How does the spatial distribution of behavioral states vary across the landscape to identify core areas and movement corridors?

Predictions:
  • Foraging/Low-energy associated with high vegetation productivity resource zones.
  • Movement concentrated along repeat-use pathways linking core areas.
  • Sleeping selected for spatially discrete areas with reduced human disturbance.
RQ 3

Demographic Variation

How do activity budgets and spatial selection differ among elephants by sex, age class, and social grouping?

Predictions:
  • Adult males exhibiting higher movement and broader spatial use, especially during musth.
  • Family groups with calves selecting for areas proximal to water and protective cover.
  • Exploratory vs. risk-averse herds showing distinct adaptation patterns in newly accessible habitat.
RQ 4

Ghost Fence Persistence

Do elephants exhibit persistent movement constraints consonant with a "ghost fence" effect following physical removal?

Predictions:
  • Initial persistence of turning/slowing near former lines with increased tortuosity.
  • Temporal decline: Strength of effect diminishes as crossings and spatial expansion increase.
  • Older individuals/socially conservative herds showing longer behavioral persistence.

Kariega Study Area

The Kariega Game Reserve, located in the Eastern Cape province of South Africa, is a premier private wildlife conservancy spanning approximately 10,000 hectares (115 km²) along the Bushmans River. The reserve is dominated by dense, woody, and thorny semi-succulent vegetation characteristic of the Albany Thicket biome.

Kariega supports a diverse assemblage of wildlife, including African elephant (Loxodonta africana), giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis), cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus), white and black rhinoceros (Ceratotherium simum and Diceros bicornis), ostrich (Struthio camelus), and numerous avian and reptile species.

Initially, the elephant population (75 individuals) was divided by an internal fence into Kariega West (KW; 2,792 ha; 55 elephants) and Harvestvale (HV; 4,369 ha; 20 elephants). Fence removal in 2024 created a contiguous 8,192 ha landscape accessible to the entire population.
